### How to Take Photos (Phone Camera)

```
SETUP (Before taking photo):

1. LIGHTING:
   - Use daylight (8am-4pm is best)
   - Avoid harsh shadows (11am-1pm can be harsh)
   - Position subject between you and light source
   - NOT backlit (light behind subject)

2. COMPOSITION:
   - Use rule of thirds (imagine 3x3 grid)
   - Put main subject on one of the lines
   - Include context (25% background)
   - Avoid clutter outside frame

3. CAMERA SETTINGS (iPhone/Android):
   - Use back camera (not selfie)
   - Lock focus (tap on subject, hold)
   - Use "Grid" to align (Settings â†’ Camera)
   - Turn OFF digital zoom (use feet to move closer)

4. ANGLE:
   - Shoot from standing height (normal viewing)
   - NOT from below (makes you look small)
   - NOT from above (unflattering)
   - Slightly angled (12-15Â°) looks better than straight

5. AFTER TAKING:
   - Take 5-10 photos, pick the best
   - Slight crop if needed (remove excess)
   - Minimal editing (don't overprocess)
   - NO fake backgrounds or filters
```
