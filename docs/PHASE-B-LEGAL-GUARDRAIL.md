## 1. CALIFORNIA BUSINESS REGULATIONS (CSLB)

### Problem
As of **January 1, 2025**, California law restricts unlicensed home service contractors:
- **Maximum contract value: $1,000** (labor + materials combined)
- **No permit work**
- **No structural modifications**
- Violation = fines up to $5,000 + criminal liability

### Handy & Friend Current Position: ‚ùì NEEDS CLARIFICATION

**DECISION REQUIRED (choose ONE):**

#### OPTION A: STAY UNDER $1,000 (No License Needed)
**Services you can offer:**
- TV Mounting (bracket provided by customer)
- Furniture Assembly (IKEA-style, simple items only)
- Art & Mirrors Hanging (up to 5 pieces)
- Weatherproofing caulk (non-structural)
- Touchups (paint refresh, same color)

**Services you CANNOT offer without CSLB:**
- ‚ùå Full wall painting (>200 sq ft is "structural modification")
- ‚ùå Flooring (installation = construction)
- ‚ùå Plumbing (even cosmetic = regulated)
- ‚ùå Electrical (any fixture swap = regulated)

**Impact on current site:**
- Current offerings mention "Painting, Flooring, Plumbing, Electrical" ‚Üí **TOO BROAD**
- Must either:
  1. **Pivot to < $1,000 services only** (TV, Furniture, Art)
  2. **Get CSLB license** (see Option B)

#### OPTION B: OBTAIN CSLB LICENSE (Contractor License)
**Requirement:** Pass exam + get bonded + insurance
**Cost:** ~$2,500-5,000 (exam, application, bond)
**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks
**Benefit:** Can offer ANY home repair service

**Action if choosing this path:**
1. Check `cslb.ca.gov` for requirement checklist
2. Get General Liability Insurance ($500-1,500/year)
3. Apply for license via CSLB portal
4. Pass exam
